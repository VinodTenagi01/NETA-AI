2. Problem Statement
Indian political campaigns â€” even well-funded, professional ones â€” are structurally broken at the intelligence layer. The following failure modes are endemic and have been directly observed across Telangana constituency campaigns:
2.1 No Centralized Campaign Intelligence
Campaign data lives across dozens of disconnected surfaces: WhatsApp group messages, shared Excel sheets, physical attendance registers, individual field worker notebooks, party office whiteboards, and verbal briefings. There is no single source of truth. When the campaign manager asks "what is happening right now in Booth 147?", the honest answer, in most campaigns, is: 
"We don't know yet."
2.2 Intelligence Latency Is Measured in Hours, Not Minutes
A voter incident at a booth at 10:00 AM may reach the campaign manager's awareness at 2:00 PM â€” after passing through a chain of WhatsApp forwards, phone calls, and informal relay. By then, the response window has closed. Vote conversion opportunities evaporate. Opposition narratives take root. NETA AI's target is to reduce this latency to 
under 60 seconds
 from field event to dashboard visibility.
2.3 Booth Management Is Intuitive, Not Scientific
Booth assignment, volunteer deployment, and contact rate tracking are managed through experience and gut feel. There is no systematic method to identify which booths are at risk, which are being neglected, or which have coverage gaps. Critical booths often receive attention only when it is too late to recover them.
2.4 News Monitoring Is Manual and Inconsistent
Campaign communications teams manually browse news portals, monitor WhatsApp forwards, and watch TV channels for political coverage. This is slow (hours behind the news cycle), inconsistent (depends on individual attention), and analytically thin (no sentiment scoring, no narrative tracking, no impact measurement). Damaging stories can circulate for hours before any campaign response is initiated.
2.5 Escalation Is Informal and Unreliable
When a critical event occurs â€” a booth dispute, a viral negative video, opposition vote-buying activity, a ground worker breakdown â€” there is no structured escalation protocol. Messages get lost in group chats. Accountability is unclear. SLA tracking is nonexistent. The result is that genuine emergencies are often managed reactively, at the worst possible moment.
2.6 Opposition Intelligence Is Reactive and Anecdotal
Most campaigns have a rough sense of what the opposition is doing based on public events and informal sources. There is no systematic tracking of opposition messaging, rally locations, media narratives, or ground deployment patterns. Campaigns discover they are losing ground narrative only when it is reflected in voter feedback â€” days or weeks too late.
2.7 Constituency Data Is Static and Unqueriable
Demographic breakdowns, caste profiles, historical vote share data, and booth-level population data exist in static PDFs, government documents, or party archives. They cannot be queried in real time, visualized spatially, or connected to live campaign activity. The constituency is effectively opaque to analytical decision-making.

15. Prediction & Sentiment Systems
15.1 Win Probability Model
The win probability model produces a constituency-level election outcome probability updated every 15 minutes throughout the campaign.
Model Formula
win_probability = sigmoid(
    w1 Ã— contact_rate_normalized
  + w2 Ã— news_sentiment_normalized
  + w3 Ã— booth_health_aggregate
  + w4 Ã— opposition_activity_penalty
  + w5 Ã— historical_vote_share
  + w6 Ã— field_mood_index
  + bias
) Ã— 100
Default weights (campaign-manager configurable):
  w1 (contact rate)       = 0.28
  w2 (news sentiment)     = 0.18
  w3 (booth health)       = 0.20
  w4 (opposition penalty) = -0.15
  w5 (historical share)   = 0.12
  w6 (field mood)         = 0.17
  bias                    = 0.10
Normalization: all inputs normalized to [0, 1] range before weighting.
Sigmoid: ensures output remains within (0%, 100%).
The model stores daily snapshots in 
intelligence_scores
 for trend visualization. A 30-day trend chart is displayed on the Command Centre.
Model Limitations (Disclosed)
The model is a heuristic approximation, not a statistical election model
Historical vote share data for Serilingampally may be incomplete for newer booth configurations
Weight calibration is based on campaign manager judgment, not empirical training data (to be improved in Phase 2 with Bayesian updating)
Adversarial events (e.g., opponent rally, negative viral story) may cause rapid score changes that should be interpreted contextually, not mechanically

21.4 SSE Architecture
Server-Sent Events power all real-time dashboard updates:
# FastAPI SSE endpoint
@router.get("/api/stream")
async def event_stream(
    request: Request,
    current_user: User = Depends(get_current_user)
):
    async def event_generator():
        pubsub = redis_client.pubsub()
        channels = get_subscribed_channels(current_user.role, current_user.zone_id)
        await pubsub.subscribe(*channels)
        try:
            while True:
                if await request.is_disconnected():
                    break
                message = await pubsub.get_message(timeout=1.0)
                if message and message['type'] == 'message':
                    yield f"data: {message['data'].decode()}\n\n"
                # Heartbeat every 30 seconds to prevent proxy timeout
                yield ": heartbeat\n\n"
                await asyncio.sleep(30)
        finally:
            await pubsub.unsubscribe(*channels)
    return StreamingResponse(
        event_generator(),
        media_type="text/event-stream",
        headers={
            "Cache-Control": "no-cache",
            "X-Accel-Buffering": "no",  # Disable Nginx buffering
        }
    )

29. Ethical Guardrails
NETA AI is a political technology platform. Ethical commitments are 
non-negotiable, hardcoded, and cannot be disabled by any user role including Super Admin
.
29.1 Information Integrity
No disinformation generation
: The AI systems do not generate false or fabricated claims about any candidate, party, or community. All AI-synthesized content is explicitly labeled as AI-generated.
Factual grounding only
: All factual claims in AI-generated briefs and alerts must be sourced to ingested data (field reports, news articles, ECI data). Unsourced claims are not generated.
Sentiment neutrality in reporting
: Sentiment analysis is applied as a measurement tool, not as a judgment. Negative sentiment flagging is for campaign awareness, not for generating attack content.
29.2 Community & Voter Protection
No communal targeting
: The platform will not generate content, analysis, or recommendations that target voters by religion, caste, or ethnicity in a discriminatory manner.
No voter suppression
: The system will not generate content, alerts, or recommendations designed to discourage any demographic from voting.
No unauthorized voter data sharing
: Voter roll data ingested into the platform is used exclusively for internal campaign coordination. It cannot be exported, shared externally, or used for commercial purposes.
29.3 Election Commission Compliance
MCC compliance module
 (Phase 2): A Model Code of Conduct check is planned for all AI-generated public-facing content. Until implemented, all AI-generated content requires human review before external publication.
No impersonation
: The system will not generate content impersonating any real individual â€” including opposition candidates.
Transparent AI labeling
: All AI-generated documents (briefs, alerts, recommendations) are labeled "AI-Generated â€” Requires Human Review" in the UI.
29.4 Data Governance
Data deletion on request
: All personally identifiable voter data and worker data can be purged on campaign manager request, consistent with applicable data protection obligations.
Retention limits
: Voter contact data is retained for 180 days post-election. Field reports and audit logs are retained for 3 years for accountability purposes.
Purpose limitation
: Data collected for campaign operations is not repurposed for commercial use, research, or future campaign targeting without explicit re-consent from data subjects.
